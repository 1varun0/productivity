# Tasks & Spaces — Feature Discovery Report

> **Audience:** Marketing, Product Design, and Growth teams
> **Last Updated:** July 2026
> **Feature Status:** Shipped & Live

---

## 1. WHAT IS THIS FEATURE — ONE LINE

**A fluid, drag-and-drop task manager that organizes your life into Spaces and turns simple to-dos into actionable deep-work sessions.**

---

## 2. THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES

### The frustration it eliminates
Most task managers suffer from two extremes. Simple lists (like Apple Reminders) get overwhelmingly cluttered. Complex tools (like Asana or Jira) require filling out five dropdown menus just to add a quick thought. More importantly, traditional task apps only tell you *what* to do, but they don't help you actually *sit down and do it*. 

### What the user was doing before
Before Tasks & Spaces, users would:
- Use a basic checklist app, letting tasks pile up until they felt "task paralysis."
- Use a complex project management tool, feeling like they were doing "work about work."
- Start a task, get distracted, and have no dedicated timer or environment to keep them accountable.

### Why it matters
This feature bridges the gap between organization and execution. It provides the frictionless capture of a simple list, the organization of a pro tool (Spaces, attachments, priorities), and seamlessly links directly to the Focus Chamber—meaning a task isn't just a checkbox, it's an invitation to do deep work.

---

## 3. EVERY CAPABILITY — COMPLETE LIST

### Spaces & Organization
- **Users can create distinct "Spaces"** — acting as lightweight folders (e.g., "Personal", "Client Work", "Groceries") to compartmentalize their life.
- **Users can reorder Spaces via drag-and-drop** — customizing the sidebar hierarchy exactly how they want it.
- **Users can view an "Overview" mode** — seeing all active tasks across all Spaces, automatically grouped and sorted.

### Task Management
- **Users can capture tasks instantly** — pressing `Cmd+K` allows them to log a thought without breaking their flow.
- **Users can mark tasks as "Priority"** — flagging high-impact items so they float to the top of the list.
- **Users can set smart due dates** — using a custom calendar that automatically labels tasks as "Today", "Tomorrow", or "2 days overdue".
- **Users can check off tasks with satisfying animations** — completed tasks don't just disappear; they fade out gracefully and move to a historical log.
- **Users can view a "Recently Completed" queue** — tasks completed within the last 2 hours remain visible so you can review what you just accomplished before they move to history.

### The Detail Panel (Rich Context)
- **Users can write detailed markdown descriptions** — adding context, meeting notes, or sub-steps directly inside the task.
- **Users can attach files via drag-and-drop** — pulling PDFs, docs, or spreadsheets directly from their desktop onto the task panel.
- **Users can paste images from their clipboard** — taking a screenshot and pasting it instantly attaches it to the task.
- **Users can view images in a native Lightbox** — clicking an attached image opens a beautiful, distraction-free preview overlay.
- **Users can save web links natively** — pasting a URL automatically creates a clean, clickable attachment card (often pulling the website's icon automatically).

### The Execution Link
- **Users can launch a Focus Session directly from a task** — clicking "Enter Focus" lets them pick a duration (25m, 45m, 90m, or Custom) and immediately transports them into the distraction-free Focus Chamber dedicated to that specific task.

---

## 4. WHAT MAKES IT UNIQUE

### vs. Todoist / TickTick
Todoist is a pure list manager. While excellent for organization, it abandons you at the execution phase. Our feature's "Enter Focus" button fundamentally changes the relationship with the task. You don't just check it off; you *engage* with it.

### vs. Notion
Notion requires you to build the database, properties, and views yourself. It's incredibly slow to add a task. Our Tasks feature is opinionated, lightning-fast, and works out of the box with zero configuration.

### The single most impressive thing
**The frictionless attachment engine combined with execution.** Opening a task, pasting a screenshot from your clipboard instantly, dropping in a URL, and then hitting `Enter Focus -> 45m`. In 3 seconds, you've gathered all your context and started a deep-work timer.

## 8. WHO WILL LOVE THIS FEATURE MOST

### 🎯 Primary: ADHD Professionals & Freelancers
People who struggle with executive dysfunction or "starting" a task. 
**Their use case:** "I get overwhelmed looking at 30 tasks. I drop them into separate Spaces so I only look at 'Client X' right now. I open the task, paste the brief in the description, and hit 'Enter Focus 25m' to force myself to just start working."

### 🎨 Secondary: Students & Researchers
People who need to attach heavy context to simple actions.
**Their use case:** "My task is 'Write Literature Review'. I attach three PDFs to the task panel and paste a link to my Nexus research note. Everything I need is right there when I sit down to write."

## 9. POWER USER MOMENTS

- **Clipboard Magic:** Taking a screenshot (Cmd+Shift+4 on Mac) and simply hitting `Cmd+V` while the task panel is open to instantly upload and attach it without saving a file to the desktop.
- **Smart Link Parsing:** Pasting a raw URL and watching the system automatically try to pull the website's favicon to make a beautiful, recognizable attachment card.
- **The "2-Hour Rule":** Checking off a task and noticing it doesn't just vanish into the ether immediately—it stays in the list for 2 hours so you can feel a sense of accomplishment during your current work session.
- **Focus Conflict Resolution:** Trying to start a focus timer while another one is running, and getting a beautifully designed mini-modal asking if you want to override the current session.
