> **Audience:** Marketing, Product Design, and Growth teams
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

## 1. WHAT IS THIS FEATURE — ONE LINE

**A lightning-fast, distraction-free markdown knowledge base with built-in templates, version history, and split-screen capabilities.**

---

## 2. THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES

### The frustration it eliminates
Modern note-taking apps force a compromise. Apps like Notion are beautiful but painfully slow and bloated. Apps like Obsidian are lightning-fast but overly technical and require a degree in folder management to use. Simple apps like Apple Notes lack the structure needed for real work. 

### What the user was doing before
Before Nexus, users would:
- Wait 5 seconds for a Notion page to load just to jot down a quick idea.
- Lose their train of thought switching between their task manager and their note app.
- Accidentally delete text and realize their simple note app doesn't have version history.
- Struggle to view a reference document and write a new document at the same time without manually resizing browser windows.

### Why it matters
Nexus brings "flow state" to writing. By combining developer-grade speed (markdown, slash commands, keyboard shortcuts) with premium consumer-grade aesthetics (glassmorphism, ambient glows), users get a "Second Brain" that actually feels good to use. Because it lives directly inside their productivity suite, the friction to capture knowledge is reduced to zero.

## 3. EVERY CAPABILITY — COMPLETE LIST

### Note Creation & Organization
- **Users can create 6 types of notes** — Standard, Checklist, Memory, Resource, Idea, and Daily Journal.
- **Users can organize notes into Custom Collections** — creating a clean, folder-like structure in the sidebar.
- **Users can add inline tags to any note** — making cross-referencing and searching effortless.
- **Users can search instantly across the entire vault** — filtering by title, content, or tags with a single keystroke.
- **Users can separate standard notes from Daily Journals** — using dedicated view modes to keep the workspace uncluttered.

### The Editor Experience
- **Users can write in fluid, distraction-free Markdown** — the editor handles formatting quietly without complex toolbars.
- **Users can use Slash Commands (`/`)** — typing a slash instantly brings up a menu to insert headings, lists, quotes, or code blocks without touching the mouse.
- **Users can use a floating format bar** — selecting text reveals a contextual menu for bold, italic, and links, exactly like Medium.
- **Users can drop files directly into the editor** — drag-and-drop or paste images and PDFs straight into the note.
- **Users can embed PDFs natively** — the system automatically generates markdown to render the PDF inline.
- **Users see a live word count** — positioned quietly at the bottom of the editor.
- **Users never have to click save** — every keystroke is auto-saved, with a tiny pulse indicator showing when data is syncing.

### Advanced Workflow (Power Features)
- **Users can split the screen horizontally or vertically** — opening multiple notes side-by-side for reference and synthesis.
- **Users can "Pin" a note as a reference** — locking it into read-only mode so they don't accidentally edit their source material while writing in another pane.
- **Users can toggle Fullscreen Mode** — hiding the entire app UI for pure, uninterrupted writing.
- **Users can access full Version History** — the system automatically saves snapshots over time.
- **Users can preview and restore past versions** — clicking a timestamp lets them read an old version and revert with one click if they made a mistake.
- **Users can share and publish notes** — generating secure links to send ideas to clients or teammates.

### Templates
- **Users can start from a curated Template Gallery** — choosing from built-in frameworks for meetings, research, or brainstorming.
- **Users can save their own Custom Templates** — turning any structure they use frequently into a reusable asset.
- **Users see beautiful, animated template previews** — with distinct icons, categories, and descriptions on hover.

## 4. WHAT MAKES IT UNIQUE

### vs. Notion
Notion is a block-based database tool. It's powerful, but it's heavy. Nexus is a pure text-first markdown editor. It loads instantly, feels lighter, and doesn't force you to think about "blocks" when you just want to write. 

### vs. Obsidian
Obsidian is built for developers and requires managing local markdown files, plugins, and graph views. Nexus provides the speed and keyboard-first nature of Obsidian but wraps it in a stunning, cloud-synced, zero-configuration UI that anyone can use immediately.

### The single most impressive thing
**The Split-Pane Reference workflow.** The ability to open a research note, hit a button to "Split Right", pin the left note as read-only, and start writing in the right note—all within a beautifully blurred, glassmorphic UI—feels like a superpower. It brings IDE-level window management to a beautiful writing app.
